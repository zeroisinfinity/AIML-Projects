 At its core, BoW is a statistical representation of a document based on the frequency of the words it contains. It is called a "bag" because it discards all information about word order, grammar, and sentence structure, focusing only on whether a word appears and how often.
 The Bag-of-Words (BoW) approach is a simple and widely used technique in Natural Language Processing (NLP) to represent text data as numerical vectors. It treats a document as an unordered "bag" or collection of its words, focusing only on the frequency of each word while completely ignoring its order, grammar, and context
